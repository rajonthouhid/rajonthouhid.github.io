# Welcome

I am **Thouhidul Islam Rajon**, a graduate student in Mechanical Engineering at Grand Valley State University and a Research Assistant working at the intersection of advanced manufacturing, additive manufacturing, thermal-fluid sciences, and sustainable engineering.

My current research focuses on the design and experimental evaluation of **3D-printed porous structures for laboratory-scale compost aeration**. This work combines porous structure design, nTopology modeling, additive manufacturing, airflow analysis, sensor-based experimentation, and performance evaluation.

I also work in the Rapid Prototyping Laboratory at Grand Valley State University, where I support students with 3D printing, laser cutting, 3D scanning, plastics prototyping, fabrication, and laboratory equipment.

I am currently exploring **Ph.D. opportunities beginning in Fall 2027** in additive manufacturing, computational modeling, advanced manufacturing, design optimization, and thermal-fluid sciences.

## Current Research

### 3D-Printed Porous Structures for Compost Aeration

My master's research investigates how 3D-printed aeration structures can improve airflow distribution in laboratory-scale composting systems. The project involves:

- Designing porous aeration structures
- Creating geometries using nTopology and CAD tools
- Fabricating components through fused deposition modeling
- Measuring airflow using experimental methods
- Monitoring temperature, carbon dioxide, relative humidity, and mass changes
- Comparing different aeration schedules and operating conditions
- Preparing computational fluid dynamics analyses
