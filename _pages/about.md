## Professional Profile

I am **Thouhidul Islam Rajon**, a graduate student in Mechanical Engineering at Grand Valley State University. My academic and professional background combines mechanical engineering, industrial and production engineering, additive manufacturing, computational modeling, experimental research, and manufacturing process improvement.

I currently work as a Research Assistant on the development and evaluation of **3D-printed porous structures for laboratory-scale compost aeration**. My work involves porous structure design, nTopology modeling, additive manufacturing, airflow measurement, experimental testing, sensor-based data collection, and computational fluid dynamics.

I also work in the Rapid Prototyping Laboratory at Grand Valley State University, where I support students with prototyping, fabrication, laboratory equipment, and manufacturing technologies.

## Current Research

My master's research focuses on developing **3D-printed aeration structures for laboratory-scale composting systems**.

The research includes:

- Designing porous aeration structures
- Creating geometries using nTopology and CAD software
- Fabricating components using fused deposition modeling
- Measuring and controlling airflow
- Monitoring temperature, carbon dioxide, relative humidity, and mass
- Comparing aerated and non-aerated composting conditions
- Evaluating different aeration schedules
- Preparing computational fluid dynamics analyses
- Documenting experimental methods and results
